PV Bifacial Simulator

24-hour IST irradiance + bifacial configuration sweep

Inputs

The app runs a 24-hour simulation for the selected IST day.

Ranges

Suggested height sweep: 0.5 m to 4.5 m (step 0.5 m).

Reason: this covers practical PV mounting clearances while keeping structural assumptions and sweep size realistic; much higher mounting is uncommon and often cost-inefficient.

Ready

Model Equations & Data Sources

Effective Irradiance

G_front = GHI * max(0.58, cos(0.8 * beta))

VF = (1 - cos(beta)) / 2

G_rear = GHI * sqrt(albedo) * VF * f_height * f_sat * bifaciality

G_eff = G_front + G_rear

Power & Energy

P_hour(kW) = (G_eff / 1000) * area * frontEff * inverterEff

Energy(kWh) = sum(P_hour)

Peak(kW) = max(P_hour)

Rear Gain(%) = sum(G_rear) / sum(GHI) * 100

Front Share(%) = sum(G_front) / sum(G_eff) * 100

Data Sources

Location: OpenStreetMap Nominatim geocoding

Irradiance: NASA POWER hourly ALLSKY_SFC_SW_DWN (normalized to Wh/m^2; equivalent W/m^2 shown for averages)

Panel model: App defaults unless user overrides panel parameters
